The Government Museum, Chennai, commonly known as the Chennai Museum or Madras Museum, is one of the oldest and most significant museums in India. Established in 1851, it is located in Egmore, Chennai, and is renowned for its extensive collections of art, archaeology, and natural history. The museum complex covers an area of 16.25 acres and features six buildings with 46 galleries, making it a major cultural and historical hub in South India.

The Director of Museum

Notable collections of this museum are the world famous South Indian bronzes, Amaravati sculptures, Tanjore (Thanjavur) armoury, inscriptions on stones and copper plates, the Dowleshwaram hoard of gold coins of Raja Raja I and Kulothunga I, the Chengam hoard of copper coins, artifacts from the Megaliths of Adichanallur, the Bruce Foote Collection of prehistoric stone implements, Roman and other artifacts from the famous site of Arikamedu (near Pondicherry) Roman artifacts. The exquisite crystal reliquaries from the Bhattiprolu Stupa and the enormous skeleton of the whale obtained on shore near Mangalore..
